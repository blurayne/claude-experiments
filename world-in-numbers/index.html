How to read this

Pick a region and a time unit at the top, and a target year on the slider. Each card shows a rough total today and a bar scaled to that category's peak over 2026–2100, so there is headroom. For a growing category the bar is the current total (lila) plus the gain to your chosen year; for a shrinking one it is what's left (lila) plus the loss by then. Those segments update live as the dashboard ticks. Below are the live born / killed rates in the unit you picked. Projections follow study-based trajectories. The est. tag marks genuinely uncertain figures. The refresh interval (1–20 s) is your choice.

How regions are estimated

Humans use each region's real population and birth/death numbers, so net growth flips sign by region (Africa and India growing fast; Europe, Russia, China and Germany shrinking). Categories tied to nature — trees, insects, wild birds, wild fish and wild mammals — are scaled by the region's share of global land area. Categories tied to people — livestock, farmed fish, pet fish and pets — are scaled by population share. These shares are rough — a region's slice, not a measured local census.

Sources & assumptions

  • Humans — ~8.30 billion; ~252 births / ~121 deaths per minute; UN projection peaks at ~10.3 billion in 2084. Regional births: India ~23.2M, Africa ~46.6M, Asia ~65.5M, China ~8.8M per year. Worldometer, UN WPP 2024, database.earth.
  • Trees — ~3.04 trillion; net loss ~10 billion/yr → ~2.3 trillion by 2100. Crowther et al., Nature (2015).
  • Insects — ~10 quintillion (10¹⁹); rough turnover + ~1%/yr decline. Smithsonian.
  • Birds — ~50 billion wild birds; rough turnover + ~0.6%/yr decline. Callaghan et al., PNAS (2021).
  • Wild fish — ~3.5 trillion ocean fish; ~1.6 trillion caught/yr; ~0.8%/yr decline under overfishing. fishcount.org.uk.
  • Farmed fish (aquaculture) — ~130 billion finfish slaughtered/yr (now more than land birds + mammals); rising fast. Mood et al. (2024).
  • Pet fish (aquarium) — >1 billion ornamental fish traded/yr; ~1.4 billion kept (rough est). Animal Welfare Institute.
  • Livestock (farm animals · Nutztiere) — ~33 billion alive; ~92 billion slaughtered/yr; rising with meat demand. Our World in Data.
  • Wild mammals (Wildtiere) — ~130 billion individuals (bats ~56 bn, rodents ~25 bn); declining. Greenspoon et al., PNAS (2023).
  • Pets (Haustiere) — ~1 billion dogs & cats (plus other companion animals); rising with pet ownership. HealthforAnimals.

Totals are anchored to mid‑2026 and extrapolated. This is an illustrative experiment, not a scientific instrument.
